What you just saw. One forward pass produced a prediction. The squared error told us how wrong we were. Backprop used the chain rule to assign each weight a share of the blame — proportional to how much that weight contributed to the answer. Each weight got nudged in the direction that reduces loss, and the next forward pass landed closer to the target. Repeat thousands of times across thousands of examples, and a network learns.
